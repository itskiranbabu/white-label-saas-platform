## Revenue Models

### 1. Subscription-Based (Most Common)

**How it works:**
- Monthly or annual recurring revenue
- Tiered pricing based on features/usage
- Predictable revenue stream

**Pros:**
- ‚úÖ Predictable cash flow
- ‚úÖ Easy to forecast
- ‚úÖ Customer retention focus
- ‚úÖ Scalable

**Cons:**
- ‚ùå Requires continuous value delivery
- ‚ùå Churn management critical
- ‚ùå Upfront revenue lower

**Example Pricing:**
```
Free:       $0/month
Starter:    $29/month
Pro:        $99/month
Enterprise: $299/month
```

**Best For:** Most SaaS businesses, B2B and B2C

---

### 2. Usage-Based Pricing

**How it works:**
- Pay per API call, storage, users, etc.
- Scales with customer usage
- Often combined with base subscription

**Pros:**
- ‚úÖ Fair pricing (pay for what you use)
- ‚úÖ Lower barrier to entry
- ‚úÖ Revenue grows with customer success
- ‚úÖ Attractive to startups

**Cons:**
- ‚ùå Unpredictable revenue
- ‚ùå Complex billing
- ‚ùå Requires usage tracking

**Example Pricing:**
```
Base:        $29/month
+ API calls: $0.001 per call
+ Storage:   $0.10 per GB
+ Users:     $5 per additional user
```

**Best For:** API platforms, infrastructure services, data-heavy apps

---

### 3. White Label Licensing

**How it works:**
- One-time or annual license fee
- Resellers rebrand and sell
- Revenue share or flat fee

**Pros:**
- ‚úÖ Large upfront payments
- ‚úÖ Partner network growth
- ‚úÖ Reduced marketing costs
- ‚úÖ Scalable through partners

**Cons:**
- ‚ùå Complex partner management
- ‚ùå Revenue share reduces margins
- ‚ùå Brand dilution risk

**Example Pricing:**
```
Setup Fee:     $5,000 one-time
Platform Fee:  $299/month
Revenue Share: 20% of reseller revenue
OR
Flat License:  $999/month (unlimited reselling)
```

**Best For:** Established platforms, agency-focused products

---

### 4. Freemium Model

**How it works:**
- Free tier with limited features
- Paid tiers unlock premium features
- Conversion funnel from free to paid

**Pros:**
- ‚úÖ Low barrier to entry
- ‚úÖ Viral growth potential
- ‚úÖ Large user base
- ‚úÖ Product-led growth

**Cons:**
- ‚ùå High infrastructure costs
- ‚ùå Low conversion rates (2-5% typical)
- ‚ùå Support burden

**Example Pricing:**
```
Free:    $0/month (limited features)
Pro:     $29/month (full features)
Team:    $99/month (collaboration)
```

**Best For:** Consumer apps, viral products, PLG strategies

---

### 5. Hybrid Model (Recommended)

**How it works:**
- Base subscription + usage fees
- Best of both worlds
- Predictable base + growth upside

**Pros:**
- ‚úÖ Predictable base revenue
- ‚úÖ Revenue grows with usage
- ‚úÖ Fair pricing
- ‚úÖ Flexible for customers

**Cons:**
- ‚ùå More complex billing
- ‚ùå Requires clear communication

**Example Pricing:**
```
Starter:    $29/month + $0.01/API call (1,000 included)
Pro:        $99/month + $0.005/API call (10,000 included)
Enterprise: $299/month + $0.001/API call (100,000 included)
```

**Best For:** Most white label SaaS platforms ‚≠ê RECOMMENDED

## Pricing Strategies

### Value-Based Pricing

**Principle:** Price based on value delivered, not cost

**How to implement:**
1. Identify customer segments
2. Quantify value for each segment
3. Price at 10-20% of value delivered
4. Test and iterate

**Example:**
```
If your platform saves customers $10,000/month in costs:
‚Üí Price at $1,000-2,000/month (10-20% of value)
```

---

### Competitive Pricing

**Principle:** Price relative to competitors

**Strategies:**
- **Premium:** 20-30% above competitors (justify with superior features)
- **Parity:** Match competitor pricing (compete on features/service)
- **Penetration:** 20-30% below competitors (gain market share)

**Research competitors:**
```
Competitor A: $99/month
Competitor B: $149/month
Competitor C: $79/month

Your pricing options:
Premium:     $179/month (best features)
Parity:      $99/month (match market)
Penetration: $59/month (gain share)
```

---

### Psychological Pricing

**Tactics:**
- **Charm Pricing:** $29 instead of $30 (feels cheaper)
- **Prestige Pricing:** $1,000 instead of $999 (premium positioning)
- **Anchoring:** Show expensive plan first (makes others seem reasonable)
- **Decoy Pricing:** Middle tier designed to make premium attractive

**Example:**
```
Basic:    $29/month  (anchor - makes Pro seem reasonable)
Pro:      $99/month  (target - most popular)
Premium:  $299/month (decoy - makes Pro attractive)
```

## Usage-Based Billing

### Metrics to Track

**Common Usage Metrics:**
1. **API Calls** - $0.001-0.01 per call
2. **Storage** - $0.10-0.50 per GB/month
3. **Users** - $5-20 per additional user
4. **Emails Sent** - $0.001-0.01 per email
5. **SMS Sent** - $0.01-0.05 per SMS
6. **Bandwidth** - $0.05-0.15 per GB
7. **Compute Time** - $0.01-0.10 per hour

### Implementation with Stripe

```typescript
// Track usage
await stripe.subscriptionItems.createUsageRecord(
  'si_xxx', // subscription item ID
  {
    quantity: 100, // number of API calls
    timestamp: Math.floor(Date.now() / 1000),
    action: 'increment',
  }
);

// Stripe automatically bills at end of period
```
